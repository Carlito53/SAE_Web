My Town

Saint-Germain-en-Laye is a French municipality located 19 km west of Paris in the Yvelines department in the ÃŽle-de-France region.
The history of the city of Saint-Germain-en-Laye was for a long time linked to the presence of the castle, which was a regular residence of the kings of France whose importance decreased nevertheless as a result of the installation of Louis XIV at Versailles, and to the forest, former royal hunting domain. At the beginning of the 21st century, it is the seat of a sub-prefecture of the Yvelines and has become a sought-after residential city, with a marked international character.
Its inhabitants are called the Saint-Germanois or Saint-Germanoise. It is the 150th most populated city in metropolitan France.
Saint-Germain-en-Laye is famous for its castle, terrace, forest, schools, shops and museums (National Archaeological Museum, PrieurÃ© (Priory) Museum, Claude-Debussy Museum).

The line from Paris to Saint-Germain-en-Laye, inaugurated in 1837, is the oldest railway line open to travellers in France. Until 1838, the city was served by stagecoaches that took charge of passengers arriving at the Pecq station, the terminus of the Saint-Lazare-Le Pecq line, because trains could not cross the difference in altitude between Le Pecq and the Terrasse de Saint-Germain.
In 1838, the construction of a second track with a railway bridge and the creation of an "atmospheric" train (abandoned in 1858) allowed the extension of the line to Saint-Germain-en-Laye.
